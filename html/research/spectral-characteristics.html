Spectral characteristics
The spectral characteristics of planetary surfaces refer to how these surfaces interact with electromagnetic radiation across different wavelengths. A typical observation spectra consists of reflection spectra, absorption spectra and thermal emission spectra. The reflection spectra are a result of sunlight reflection off the planetary surface and gives a continuum slope. The thermal emission corresponds to the native temperature of the planet. Absorption spectra are marked by dips in the continuum and are characteristic of specific elements and compounds present in the planetary surface and atmosphere.
Absorption
Some elements in the atmosphere absorb part of the spectrum and they occur as dips in the data
Emission
Some elements in the atmosphere emit certain wavelengths and they occur as peaks in the data
Continous
Produced due to sunlight reflecting of the atmosphere and the absorption and emission sits on it
Requirements
Resolution of the absorption spectra depends on the resolution of the spectrograph. Faint absorption spectra require a high-resolution spectrograph since they can be subdued by instrumental (especially thermal) and background noises. A combination of a sophisticated spectrograph and robust statistical methods can lead to very interesting discoveries; This will also facilitate collaboration and knowledge exchange programs
