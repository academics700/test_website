Space debris
Space debris refers to defunct human-made objects in space, primarily in Earth's orbit. These objects include non-functional satellites, spent rocket stages, fragments from spacecraft, and other debris resulting from collisions or disintegration events. These debris can vary in shape and size
Importance
Imaging space debris helps track the location, trajectory, and characteristics of objects in orbit. This information is crucial for space traffic management, enabling satellite operators and space agencies to plan satellite launches, maneuvers, and avoid potential collisions. They pose a significant risk to operational satellites, spacecraft, and even crewed missions in space. Collisions with space debris can cause catastrophic damage due to the high velocities involved, creating even more debris.

Our contribution
With our 20 – inch telescope with automated tracking and adaptive optic system, we will be able to image and track faint space debris using their relative position with respect to other stars, planets and planetary satellites. This will enable us to produce our own catalog of space debris which can then assist international space agencies in effectively tracking and removing space debris to decrease the threat of collisions.
